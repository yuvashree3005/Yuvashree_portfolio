Customization for the Audience: I designed this artifact to make neural networks understandable for both technical peers and non-technical banking leaders. The slides use clear diagrams and concise explanations so that stakeholders can see how inputs, layers, and outputs relate to real decisions such as risk scoring or fraud detection.

Artifact 8 Reflection: Training Generative AI Large Language Models – Process & Resource Costs

Customization for the Audience: I designed this artifact to clearly communicate the essentials of training large language models for a wide audience, including technical learners, business leaders, and decision-makers. The infographic separates the training workflow from the resource requirements so viewers can easily understand both the process and the scale of investment needed for models like GPT-4, Claude, Gemini, and LLaMA.

Lessons Learned: Developing this artifact deepened my understanding of the massive infrastructure behind generative AI. I gained more clarity on how trillions of tokens, high-performance GPU clusters, energy consumption, and weeks of continuous training all contribute to the capabilities of modern LLMs. This experience reinforced the importance of responsible AI development, especially regarding cost, efficiency, and environmental impact.

Feedback and Revisions: Using feedback from earlier artifacts, I focused heavily on clarity, readability, and visual hierarchy. I chose a professional blue-and-white layout, simple icons, and clean spacing to make the infographic easy to present and discuss with executives. These refinements strengthened my ability to communicate complex AI infrastructure topics in a concise and accessible format—an important skill for guiding AI adoption in banking and other industries.
