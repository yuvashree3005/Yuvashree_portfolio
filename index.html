Artifact 2 Reflection: Power BI Dashboard for Customer Insights

This dashboard project taught me how to transform data into meaningful insights for business leaders. I tailored the dashboard to banking needs by segmenting customers by value and churn risk. Feedback from peers led me to enhance visualization clarity by adjusting filters and categories. I learned how to create executive-ready dashboards that drive decision-making — a skill that directly strengthens my leadership ability in financial services.

Artifact 3 Reflection: Chatbot-Based Learning of ML Concepts

Using ChatGPT to learn about machine learning enhanced both my technical understanding and my ability to explain technical concepts. I designed the learning session to be beginner-friendly, using real-world examples to illustrate supervised and unsupervised learning. Feedback encouraged me to enrich examples and draw clearer parallels to everyday scenarios. This artifact demonstrated my ability to self-learn complex topics and translate them into simple, digestible educational materials.

Artifact 4 Reflection: Machine Learning Model for Customer Churn Prediction

This project tied directly into my career goals in retail banking leadership. I built predictive models to identify customer churn risks, customizing features based on business relevance. Feedback highlighted the importance of including customer behavior signals like tenure and complaints, which I incorporated. This artifact showcased my ability to create real-world AI models that align with business needs and improve retention strategies — key for leadership roles.

Artifact 5 Reflection: Business Case for AI-Driven Personalization in Retail Banking

This artifact demonstrated my ability to align AI strategies with tangible business goals. I built a structured business case that connected AI personalization technologies to customer satisfaction, cross-selling opportunities, and churn reduction. Feedback emphasized the need to highlight ethical risks, which I integrated into my recommendations. This artifact shows I can communicate the value of AI investment to senior executives while balancing innovation with responsible AI governance.

Artifact 6 Reflection: Algorithm Framework and Applications in Machine Learning

Customization for the Audience: I built this artifact for both academic reviewers and professional stakeholders. The visual layout is designed to be readable for non-technical leaders, while the detailed write-up supports technical evaluation. I intentionally connected each algorithm to real business decisions, including fraud monitoring, customer segmentation, and automated reporting.

Lessons Learned: Creating this artifact helped me clearly see how supervised learning, unsupervised learning, and generative AI solve different classes of problems. I learned how traditional models like Random Forest and modern models like Transformers each support different parts of an organization: prediction, grouping, language understanding, and content generation.

Feedback and Revisions: My previous portfolio feedback emphasized clarity and audience relevance. I applied that here by pairing a clean infographic with a structured written explanation and reflection. The result shows not just that I understand the algorithms, but that I can communicate them in a way that supports leadership and decision-making.
